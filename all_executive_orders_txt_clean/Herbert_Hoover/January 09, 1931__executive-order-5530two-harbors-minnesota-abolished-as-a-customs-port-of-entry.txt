Executive Order 5530—Two Harbors, Minnesota, Abolished as a Customs Port of Entry
January 09, 1931
Herbert Hoover

By virtue of the authority vested in me by the act of Congress approved August 1, 1914 (38 Stat. 623), making appropriations for the sundry civil expenses of the Government for the fiscal year ended June 30, 1915, Two Harbors, Minn., is hereby abolished as a customs port of entry in Customs Collection District No. 36 (Duluth and Superior), with headquarters at Duluth, Minn., and Superior, Wis., effective 30 days from the date of this order.

HERBERT HOOVER

The White House,

January 9, 1931.
