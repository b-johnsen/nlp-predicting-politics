Executive Order 7664—Modifying the Seney Migratory Waterfowl Refuge
July 17, 1937
Franklin D. Roosevelt

MICHIGAN

By virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me as President of the United States and by the act of June 25, 1910, ch. 421, 36 Stat. 847, as amended by the act of August 24, 1912, ch. 369, 37 Stat. 497, and In order to effectuate further the purposes of the Migratory Bird Conservation Act (45 Stat. 1222) it is ordered as follows:

Section 1. All lands owned or controlled by the United States within the following-described area in Michigan, subject to any existing valid rights, are hereby included in and reserved as a part of the Seney Migratory Waterfowl Refuge, established by Executive Order No. 7246 of December 10, 1935:

Provided

, that any private lands within such area shall become a part of the Refuge upon the acquisition of title thereto or lease thereof by the United States:

Section 3. The lands excluded from the said Refuge by section 2 hereof shall be and remain under the control and jurisdiction of the Secretary of Agriculture.

Section 4. The reservation made by section 1 of this order supersedes as to the lands described therein the withdrawal made by Executive Order No. 6964 of February 5, 1935, as amended, for classification and other purposes.

FRANKLIN D ROOSEVELT

The White House,

July 17, 1937.
