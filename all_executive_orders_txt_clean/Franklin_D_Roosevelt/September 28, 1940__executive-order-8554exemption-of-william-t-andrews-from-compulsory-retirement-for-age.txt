Executive Order 8554—Exemption of William T. Andrews From Compulsory Retirement for Age
September 28, 1940
Franklin D. Roosevelt

Whereas

, in my judgment, the public interest requires that William T. Andrews, Valuation Expert, Department of Justice, who as Engineer Revenue Agent, Bureau of Internal Revenue, Treasury Department, was exempted from compulsory retirement for age for a period of one year ending September 30, 1940, by Executive Order No. 8228 of August 24, 1939, be further exempted from such compulsory retirement for a period of one year:

Now, Therefore

, by virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me by Section 204 of the Act of June 30, 1932, 47 Stat. 382, 404 (U.S.C., Title 5, Section 715a), I hereby further exempt the said William T. Andrews from compulsory retirement for age for a period of one year ending September 30, 1941.

FRANKLIN D ROOSEVELT

The White House,

September 28, 1940.
