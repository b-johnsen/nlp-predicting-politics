Executive Order 7650—Establishing Moosehorn Migratory Bird Refuge
July 01, 1937
Franklin D. Roosevelt

MAINE

By virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me as President of the United States, and in order to effectuate further the purposes of the Migratory Bird Conservation Act (45 Stat. 1222), it is ordered that the lands and waters acquired or to be acquired by the United States within the following-described boundary, comprising approximately 11,949 acres in Washington County, Maine, be, and they are hereby, reserved and set apart, subject to existing valid rights, for the use of the Department of Agriculture as a refuge and breeding ground for migratory birds and other wildlife:

Provided

, That any private lands within the area described shall become a part of the refuge hereby established upon the acquisition of title thereto or lease thereof by the United States:
