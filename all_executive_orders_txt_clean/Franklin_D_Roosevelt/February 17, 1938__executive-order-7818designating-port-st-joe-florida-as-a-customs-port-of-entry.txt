Executive Order 7818—Designating Port St. Joe, Florida, as a Customs Port of Entry
February 17, 1938
Franklin D. Roosevelt

By virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me by the act of August 1, 1914, 38 Stat. 609, 623 (U.S.C., title 19, sec. 2), it is ordered that the port of Port St. Joe, Florida, be, and it is hereby, designated as a customs port of entry in Customs Collection District No. 18 (Florida), with headquarters at Tampa, Florida.

This order shall become effective thirty days from the date hereof.

FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT

The White House,

February 17, 1938.
