3.
Providing
volunteer opportunities for Federal employees engaged in STEM
activities
The Office of
Personnel Management, in consultation with the Office of Science and Technology
Policy and the Department of Education, shall develop a policy toâ
(1)
increase volunteerism in STEM education
activities by encouraging Federal employees engaged in STEM activities,
including scientists, engineers, and cybersecurity specialists engaged in
nonmilitary activities, from Federal agencies and the federally funded research

education activities by providing administrative leave and support for such

(2)
support increased
communication and partnerships between scientists and engineers from Federal
science agencies conducting nonmilitary scientific research and development,
including scientists and engineers of the federally funded research and
development centers supported by those agencies, and elementary and secondary
schools and teachers through volunteerism in STEM education activities.
4.
Conducting
outreach to underrepresented communities
Each Federal science agency shall report to
Congress the approaches that it will take to conduct outreach designed to
promote widespread public understanding of career opportunities in the STEM
fields specific to the workforce needs of that agency, including outreach to
women, Latinos, African-Americans, Native Americans, and other students from
groups underrepresented in STEM fields.
