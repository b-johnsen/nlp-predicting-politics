Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Crimea Annexation Non-recognition Act
.
2.
Prohibition against United States recognition of Russiaâs annexation of Crimea
(a)
Statement of policy
It is the policy of the United States not to recognize the de jure or de facto sovereignty of the
Russian Federation over Crimea, its airspace, or its territorial waters.
(b)
Prohibition
In accordance with subsection (a), no Federal department or agency may take any action or extend
any assistance that recognizes or implies recognition of the de jure or de
facto sovereignty of the Russian Federation over Crimea, its airspace, or
its territorial waters.
(c)
Waiver
The President may waive subsection (a) or (b) if the President determines that it is vital to the
national security interests of the United States to do so.
