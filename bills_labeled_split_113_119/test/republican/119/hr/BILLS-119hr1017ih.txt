2.
Prohibition
(a)
In general
Notwithstanding any other provision of law and except as provided in subsection (b), Federal funds may not be provided, directly or indirectly, to an entity if such entity permits an individual to access or use a single-sex facility on the property of such entity that does not correspond to the biological sex of such person, except as provided by subsection (c).

(2)

(c)
Definitions
In this section:
(1)
Biological sex
The term
biological sex
means the biological determination as to whether an individual is male or female.
(2)
Entity
The term
entity
means any private entity, non-Federal Government agency or department, or State, Tribal, or local government (including a political subdivision, department, or component thereof).
(3)
Female
The term
female
means an individual who naturally has, had, will have, or would have, but for a developmental or genetic anomaly or historical accident, the reproductive system that at some point produces, transports, and uses eggs for fertilization.
(4)
Male
The term
male
means an individual who naturally has, had, will have, or would have, but for a developmental or genetic anomaly or historical accident, the reproductive system that at some point produces, transports, and uses sperm for fertilization.
(5)
Property
The term
property
means any building, land, or other real property owned, leased, controlled, or occupied by an entity.
