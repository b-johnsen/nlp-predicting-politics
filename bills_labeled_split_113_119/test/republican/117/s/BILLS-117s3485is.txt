1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Securing Enfranchisement and the Rights of Voters regardless of Inoculation Status Act

2.
Right to vote shall not be impaired
(a)
In general
It shall be unlawful for any State or political subdivision, as such term is used in the Voting Rights Act of 1965 (
52 U.S.C. 10301 et seq.
), to require or impose a requirement that a voter or voters must present a vaccine passport or otherwise present information regarding their vaccination status in order to exercise the right to vote, including to vote in person, in any election involving any candidate for Federal office.
