Whereas the Property Clause of the Constitution (article IV, section 3, clause 2) vests in Congress the power to make needful rules and regulations respecting the property belonging to the United States;
Whereas section 204(c) of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976 (
43 U.S.C. 1714(c)
) provides that a withdrawal by the Secretary of the Interior of 5,000 acres or more shall terminate and become ineffective
if the Congress has adopted a concurrent resolution stating that such House does not approve the withdrawal
; and
Whereas, on January 31, 2023, the Secretary of the Interior issued Public Land Order No. 7917 (88 Fed. Reg. 6308) that withdrew approximately 225,504 acres of National Forest System land in Cook, Lake, and Saint Louis Counties, Minnesota, from disposition under the United States mineral and geothermal leasing laws: Now, therefore, be it
That Congress disapproves of the withdrawal by the Secretary of the Interior of approximately 225,504 acres of National Forest System lands in Cook, Lake, and Saint Louis Counties, Minnesota, from disposition under the United States mineral and geothermal leasing laws.
