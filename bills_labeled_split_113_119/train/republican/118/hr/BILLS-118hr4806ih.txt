1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Protection of American Land Act of 2023
.
2.
Prohibition against foreign nations owning certain facilities, buildings, and land
(a)
Prohibition
Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a foreign nation may notâ
(1)
establish a police or State sponsored security facility;
(2)
own or purchase a State sponsored government building in the United States; or
(3)
own or purchase land in the United States.
(b)
Compensation provided
Notwithstanding any other provision of law, if a foreign nation owns a facility, building, or land described in subsection (a), such foreign nation shall be provided market-rate compensation for such facility, building, or property within two years of the date of the enactment of this Act by the Secretary of Treasury.
(c)
Return required
Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a foreign nation owning a facility, building, or land described in subsection (a) such foreign nation shall return such facility, building, or land to the United States Government.
(d)
No liability
Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the United States Government shall not be liable for any violation of contracts that occur as a result of this Act.
(e)
Definitions
In this section:
(1)
Foreign nation
The term
foreign nation
includes a foreign nation, a State sponsored entity, or a government official of a foreign nation.
(2)
Police or State sponsored security facility
The term
police or State sponsored security facility
means a facility that conducts security, espionage, acts of law enforcement, or surveillance on United States citizens.
(3)
State sponsored government building
The term
State sponsored government building
means a building owned by a foreign nation.
