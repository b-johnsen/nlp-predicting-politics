301.
Rulemaking related to stress capital buffer requirements
(a)
In general
Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System (in this title referred to as the
Board
) shall issue a rule to establish any models, assumptions, formulas, or other decisional methodologies that are used to determine any component or subcomponent of the stress capital buffer requirement for a bank holding company.
(b)
Changes
The Board may only make changes to the rule issued under subsection (a) through notice and comment rulemaking.
(c)
Stress capital buffer requirement defined
The term
stress capital buffer requirement
has the meaning given the term in section 225.8(c) of title 12 of the Code of Federal Regulations.
302.
Rulemaking relating to stress testing
(a)
In general
Beginning in the first calendar year after the date of the enactment of this section, the Board shall, not less than 30 days before conducting a stress test pursuant to section 165(i) of the Financial Stability Act of 2010, issue a rule to establish each scenario to be used in such stress test.
(b)
Prohibition
The Board may not, by rule or otherwise, subject any nonbank financial company to a climate-related stress test using the authority provided in section 165(i) of the Financial Stability Act of 2010.
303.
GAO report
(a)
In general
The Comptroller General of the United States shall, every 3 years, conduct a study and submit a report to the Congress with respect to the stress tests conducted by the Board under section 165(i) of the Financial Stability Act of 2010 in the 3 most recent calendar years.
(b)
Contents
The report submitted to the Congress under subsection (a) shall芒聙聰

(2)
consider whether the stress tests adequately identify salient risks to芒聙聰

(B)
Contents

(i)

(i)

(ii)
timelines and milestones for implementing the plan and measures to demonstrate how the Board of Governors will maintain implemented improvements on an ongoing basis; and
(iii)
measures of managing and controlling deficiencies until the plan is implemented in full.
(E)

In general
