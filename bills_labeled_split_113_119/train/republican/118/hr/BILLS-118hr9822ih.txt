(b)
Findings
Congress finds the following:
(1)
Brain health is defined as the biological, neurological, and physical characteristics of the brain affecting a person芒聙聶s health and demeanor.
(2)
Terminology such as
mental health
or
mental illness
can have negative connotations that deter children from seeking treatment. Using
brain health
or
brain illness
instead can help reduce the stigma and encourage seeking treatment.
(3)
Children who have their brain health needs met tend to have positive outcomes regarding their social and academic development. Addressing brain health can enhance their ability to focus, learn, and interact positively with peers and adults.
(4)
Over 1 in 5 children have a brain health condition, yet the majority do not receive the necessary assistance.
(5)
It is critical for Congress to take steps to reduce the stigma associated with brain health conditions and find ways to encourage children and their families to seek access to brain health treatments and preventative care.
