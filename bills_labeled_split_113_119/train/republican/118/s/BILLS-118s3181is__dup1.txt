1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Michel O. Maceda Memorial Act
.
2.
Findings
Congress makes the following findings:
(1)
Marine Interdiction Agent Michel O. Maceda served honorably in the United States Army as a wheeled vehicle mechanic prior to joining the United States Border Patrol as an Agent in Douglas, Arizona, in April 2016.
(2)
In 2021, Agent Maceda became a Marine Interdiction Agent with U.S. Customs and Border Protection, Air and Marine Operations in MayagÃ¼ez, Puerto Rico.
(3)
On November 17, 2022, Agent Maceda and 2 other agents were conducting a drug interdiction stop off the coast of Puerto Rico, during which the agents boarded the target vessel.
(4)
During the stop, Agent Maceda was mortally wounded when 1 of the occupants of the vessel shot the boarding agents with a firearm.
(5)
Agent Maceda is survived by his daughter, brother, and parents.
3.
Designation
The Air and Marine Operations Marine Unit of the U.S. Customs and Border Protection located at 101 Km 18.5 in Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico, shall forever be known and designated as the
Michel O. Maceda Marine Unit
.
4.
References
Any reference in a law, map, regulation, document, paper, or other record of the United States to the marine unit referred to in section 3 shall be deemed to be a reference to the
Michel O. Maceda Marine Unit
.
