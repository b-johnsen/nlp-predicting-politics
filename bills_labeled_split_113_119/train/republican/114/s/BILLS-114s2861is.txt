1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder Medication Prescribing Improvement Act of 2016
.
2.
Requirement to review and monitor prescribing practices at military treatment facilities of
pharmaceutical agents for treatment
of post-traumatic stress
(a)
In general
Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the Secretary of Defense shallâ
(1)
conduct a comprehensive review of the prescribing practices at military treatment facilities of pharmaceutical agents for the treatment of post-traumatic stress;
(2)
implement a process or processes to monitor the prescribing practices at military treatment facilities of pharmaceutical agents that are discouraged from use under the VA/DOD Clinical Practice Guideline for Management of Post-Traumatic Stress; and
(3)
implement a plan to address any deviations from such guideline in prescribing practices of pharmaceutical agents for management of post-traumatic stress at such facilities.
(b)
Pharmaceutical agent defined
In this section, the term
pharmaceutical agent
has the meaning given that term in section 1074g(g) of title 10, United States Code.
