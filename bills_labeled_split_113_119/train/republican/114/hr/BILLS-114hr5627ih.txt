1.
Findings
Congress finds the following:
(1)
The monarch butterfly is almost certainly the most widely recognized of all butterflies in the United States.
(2)
The monarch butterfly population is widely distributed across the United States, from Maine to Hawaii.
2.
Establishment and adoption of the monarch butterfly as the national butterfly
(a)
In general
The insect commonly known as the
monarch butterfly
(Danaus plexippus) is adopted as the national butterfly of the United States.
(b)
Rule of construction
Nothing in this Act or the adoption of the monarch butterfly as the national butterfly of the United States shall be construed or used as a reason to alter, change, modify, or otherwise affect any plan, policy, management decision, regulation, or other action by the Federal Government.
