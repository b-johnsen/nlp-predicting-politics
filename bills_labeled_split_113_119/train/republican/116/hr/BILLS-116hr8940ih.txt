1.
Prohibiting appointment of interns who are citizens of countries exerting malign foreign influences
(a)
Prohibition
An office of Congress, including an office of a joint committee of Congress, may not appoint an individual as a paid or unpaid intern if the individual is a citizen of a covered foreign country.
(b)
Definitions

(2)

intern
means, with respect to an office of Congress, an individual who serves in the office for not more than 120 days in a 12-month period and whose service is primarily for the educational experience of the individual.
(c)
Effective date
This section shall apply with respect to the One Hundred Seventeenth Congress and each succeeding Congress.
