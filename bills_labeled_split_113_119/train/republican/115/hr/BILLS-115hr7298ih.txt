Short title

Connect America's Capital Act
.
2.
Findings
Congress finds the following:
(1)
The bipartisan Airline Deregulation Act of 1978, signed into law by President Jimmy Carter, and the subsequent explosive growth in American commercial aviation has proven that it is counterproductive for Congress to interfere with market forces and restrict where airlines can and cannot fly.
(2)
Advancements in technology, such as Stage 4 and Stage 5 Aircraft, as well as the steady growth of enplanements and passenger traffic at Dulles International Airport, make arguments for the perimeter rule less and less relevant.
(3)
Residents of the District of Columbia, Maryland, and Virginia should have more options, not less, when choosing direct flights and where they can fly.
(4)
American citizens wishing to visit Washington, DC, deserve to be better connected to the nationâs capital.
(5)
Consumers and airlines, not Members of Congress, should determine airline routes.
