2.
Prohibition on domestic intelligence activities by the intelligence community
Title I of the National Security Act of 1947 (
50 U.S.C. 3021 et seq.
) is amended by inserting before section 101 the following new section (and conforming the table of contents accordingly):
100.
Prohibition on domestic intelligence activities by the intelligence community
(a)
Prohibition
Except as provided by subsections (b) and (c), notwithstanding any other provision of law, an element of the intelligence community may not conduct any intelligence or intelligence-related activity unless such activity has a nexus to foreign intelligence or counterintelligence.
(b)
Exception
The prohibition in subsection (a) shall not apply with respect to the administrative and human resources functions of an element of the intelligence community.
(c)
Waiver
