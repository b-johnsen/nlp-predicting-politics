1.
World Health Organization
(a)
Effect of World Health Organization directives and orders on the United States
Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the United States shall not be bound by any directive or order issued by the World Health Assembly, the World Health Organization, or any agent or branch of the World Health Organization.
(b)
Opposition to amendments to the Constitution of the World Health Organization and any other agreements among member states without congressional approval
The Secretary of Health and Human Services and any other United States official designated by the President to represent the United States at the World Health Organization in any capacity shall use the voice, vote, and influence of the United States to oppose any amendments to the Constitution of the World Health Organization and any other agreements among the member states unless Congress has enacted a joint resolution in support of such amendments.
