1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) Impact Act
.
2.
Finding
Congress finds that, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, each year in the United States, respiratory syncytial virus leads to nearly 2,100,000 outpatient visits from children under the age of 5 years old, leading to approximately 58,000 hospitalizations within this population.
3.
Study on RSV transmission
The Secretary of Health and Human Services shall seek to enter into a contract with the National Academy of Sciences, under which such National Academy studies respiratory syncytial virus transmission in schools and daycare facilities and the impact such virus has on the economy, including through parents' absences from work, and reports to Congress on the findings of such study.
