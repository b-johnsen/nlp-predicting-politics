Article II
President Bidenâs decision to liquidate the SPR reveals his plan to endanger the security of the United States, both economically and politically, while empowering adversaries to do grave harm to us and our allies.
Congress authorized the creation of the SPR in 1975 under the Energy Policy and Conservation Act.
By December 1994, the SPR had been filled to 591,700,000 barrels.
In mid-November of 2001, President George W. Bush ordered the SPR filled to 700,000,000 barrels.
The SPR was filled to its then 727-million-barrel authorized storage capacity on December 27, 2009; the inventory of 726,600,000 barrels was the highest ever held in the SPR.
The SPRâs current authorized storage capacity is 714,000,000 barrels of crude oil.
When President Joe Biden entered office, the SPR contained 638,000,000 barrels of crude oil. In 2021, the United States consumed an average of 19,780,000 barrels of petroleum per day, a total of 7,220,000,000 barrels of petroleum.
In 2021, 134,830,000,000 gallons of finished motor gasoline was consumed in the United States, an average of about 369,000,000 gallons per day.
In 2021, diesel fuel consumption by the United States transportation sector was 46,820,000,000 gallons, an average of about 128,000,000 gallons per day.
President Biden announced on March 31, 2022, that his administration would begin releasing at least 1,000,000 barrels of oil from the SPR on a daily basis, with a target total of more than 180,000,000 barrels of oil.
The national average price of unleaded gasoline and diesel fuel in June 2022, was $5, an all-time high.
The SPR currently holds 427,200,000 barrels of crude oil as of September 16, 2022. This is after Reuters reported in August that the SPR
fell to the lowest level since 1984
.
No President in the history of the United States has ordered a liquidation of the SPR of this scale. Even in 1991, during the Gulf war, the United States liquidated only 17,000,000 barrels of crude oil from the SPR to provide further stability to the global crude oil market. Following Hurricane Katrina, the United States released some 20,000,000 barrels of crude oil to the United States domestic market following the destruction of major oil refineries in the gulf coast region.
In 2021, the Department of Energy sold crude oil from the SPR to a foreign government for the first time.
President Biden is emptying the SPR of medium-sour crude oil, which is a crucial type of oil that domestic refiners process into gasoline. The Department of Energy released 4,600,000 barrels of medium-sour crude oil in late July. This decision could cause oil prices to skyrocket in the long-term as refineries seek to re-acquire more easily processed crude.

President Bidenâs decision to deplete the SPR is unnecessary, since the United States had already materially demonstrated that it was energy-independent and fully capable of producing sufficient amounts of crude oil domestically under the Trump administration.
