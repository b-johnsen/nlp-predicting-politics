Whereas Anna Mae Hays was born in Buffalo, New York, on February 16, 1920, to her parents Daniel Joseph McCabe and the former Mattie Humphrey;
Whereas as a young woman growing up in Allentown, Pennsylvania, Hays practiced nursing by tying bandages around the legs of a kitchen table where her parents, Salvation Army officers, often invited the ill to eat dinner;
Whereas at the age of 22 in May 1942, Hays joined the United States Army in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, to serve as a nurse during World War II;
Whereas Hays deployed to Assam, India, where she helped treat some of the more than 49,000 patients taken to the 20th General Hospital, including troops injured in combat in Burma, and those who required amputations inside operating rooms constructed from bamboo;
