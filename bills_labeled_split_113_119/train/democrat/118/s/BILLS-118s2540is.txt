(7)
Waiver authority
(A)
Definition of covered regulation
The term
covered regulation
means a regulation issued by the Secretary pursuant to this subsection on determination by the Secretary that the regulation is necessary or appropriate for the effective and efficient administration of the food distribution program on Indian reservations under this subsection.
(B)
Purpose
The purpose of this paragraph is to facilitate the effective operation of the food distribution program on Indian reservations under this subsection in partnership with Tribal organizations and State agencies.
(C)
Waiver
(i)
In general
The Secretary may waive a covered regulation on the request of an Indian Tribe.
(ii)
Request
An Indian Tribe shall request a waiver under clause (i) through the Office of Tribal Relations, which shall consult with any implicated agency regarding the waiver.
(iii)
Determination
(I)
In general
Not later than 30 days after receiving a request under clause (ii), the Secretary shall respond in writing with a determination.
(II)
Denial
If the Secretary determines under subclause (I) that the waiver is denied, the Secretary shallâ
(aa)
include in the written response any curative adjustments necessary to approve the waiver; and
(bb)
permit the Indian Tribe to resubmit a waiver request with those curative adjustments not later than 30 days after the receipt of the response of the Secretary under subclause (I).
(III)
Subsequent approval
The Secretary shall grant a waiver to an Indian Tribe not later than 30 days after determining that a resubmitted request under subclause (II)(bb) satisfactorily cures the reasons for the initial denial of the request.

(B)
Nutrition education funding
If appropriated funds are not available, Tribal organizations and State agencies may use to carry out subparagraph (A) nutrition education funds otherwise provided by the Secretary for the food distribution program on Indian reservations under this subsection.
(C)
Nutrition education programming
Tribal organizations shall have broad discretion in determining the format and content of nutrition education programming provided to recipients of food distributed under the food distribution program on Indian reservations under this subsection.
