(10)

(A)

Except in emergencies and for closures issued for culturally affiliated Indian Tribes under paragraph (2)(B), the Secretary shall consult with the appropriate State agency before issuing orders under clause (i) that restrict or prohibit hunting or fishing within the Monument.
(C)

Management activities (including the use of motorized vehicles by appropriate State agencies) to maintain, enhance, or restore fish and wildlife populations and the habitats to support fish and wildlife populations may be carried out within the Monument, subject to applicable laws (including regulations).

4.
Management plan
(a)
In general
Not later than 2 years after the date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary shall begin developing a management plan for the Monument.
(b)
Tribal consultation
The Secretary shall consult with culturally affiliated Indian Tribes with respect toâ
(1)
the development of the management plan under subsection (a); and
(2)
management decisions relating to the Monument.
(c)
Continued engagement with culturally affiliated indian tribes
The management plan shall set forth parameters for continued meaningful engagement with culturally affiliated Indian Tribes with respect to the implementation of the management plan.
(d)
Ensuring access to dwellings contained in private inholdings
The Secretary shall consult with owners of cabins at Medicine Lake to ensure that continued access relating to the use of the cabins is addressed in the management plan.
(e)
Wildfire
The management plan shall include a fire management plan that may provide for prescribed fires, including traditional indigenous burning practices and thinning conducted by a culturally affiliated Indian Tribe to address forest health and safety.
(f)
Effect
Nothing in this Act affects the conduct of fire prevention, mitigation, suppression, or cleanup activities associated with wildfire, forest health, and fuels reduction within the Monument, including through the use of existing agreements.
