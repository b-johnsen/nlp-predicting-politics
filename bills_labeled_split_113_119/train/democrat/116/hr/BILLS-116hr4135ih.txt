1.
Removal of Statue of Albert Pike
(a)
Findings
Congress finds as follows:
(1)
The Supreme Council of the Inspectors-General of the Thirty-Third Degree of the Ancient and Accepted Scottish Rite of Freemasonry for the Southern Jurisdiction of the United States of America supports the removal of the statue to the memory of Albert Pike erected near Judiciary Square in the District of Columbia due to its divisive nature.
(2)
The Mayor of the District of Columbia and the Council of the District of Columbia support the removal of the Albert Pike statue from its current location.
(b)
Removal
After non-Federal funds are made available for this purpose, the Secretary of the Interior shall remove the statue to the memory and in honor of Albert Pike erected near Judiciary Square in the District of Columbia under the
Joint Resolution Granting permission for the erection of a monument or statue in Washington City, District of Columbia, in honor of the late Albert Pike.
, approved April 9, 1898 (30 Stat. 737).
(c)
No use of Federal funds
No Federal funds may be used to carry out this Act.
