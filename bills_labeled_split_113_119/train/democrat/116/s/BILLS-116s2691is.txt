(e)
Appointment of Border Communities Liaison
(1)
In general
The Ombudsman, in conjunction with the Office for Civil Rights and Civil Liberties of the Department, shall appoint a Border Community Liaison (referred to in this subsection as a

(f)

(1)

The Ombudsman shall establish a Border Oversight Panel (referred to in this subsection as the

(2)
Composition
(A)

The Panel shall be composed of 30 members selected by the Ombudsman.
(B)
Chairperson
The Ombudsman shall serve as the chair of the Panel.
(C)
Expertise
Members of the Panel shall have expertise in immigration, local crime indices, civil and human rights, community relations, cross-border trade and commerce, quality of life indicators, or other experience the Ombudsman determines is appropriate, and shall include individuals who reside in or near border counties.

(B)

(C)
to reduce the number of migrant deaths; and
(D)
to improve the safety of agents and officers of U.S. Customs and Border Protection and U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement.
