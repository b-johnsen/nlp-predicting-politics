Executive Order
December 21, 1898
William McKinley

ADJUTANT-GENERAL'S OFFICE

Washington,

General Otis,

Manila:

Answering your message of December 14, the President directs that you send necessary troops to Iloilo to preserve the peace and protect life and property. It is most important that there should be no conflict with the insurgents. Be conciliatory, but firm.

By order of the Secretary War:

CORBIN.
