Executive Order 5475—Lake Charles, Louisiana, Designated a Customs Port of Entry
November 03, 1930
Herbert Hoover

By virtue of the authority vested in me by the act of Congress approved August 1, 1914 (38 Stat. 623), making appropriations for the sundry civil expenses of the Government for the fiscal year ended June 30, 1915, the customs station of Lake Charles, La., is hereby designated a customs port of entry in Customs Collection District No. 21 (Sabine) with headquarters at Port Arthur, Tex., effective 30 days from the date of this order.

HERBERT HOOVER

The White House,

November 3, 1930.
