Executive Order 7699—Abolishing Nome, Alaska, as a Customs Port of Entry
August 28, 1937
Franklin D. Roosevelt

By virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me by the act of August 1, 1914, 38 Stat. 609, 623 (U.S.C., title 19, sec. 2), it is ordered that Nome, Alaska, be, and it is hereby, abolished as a customs port of entry in Customs Collection District No. 31 (Alaska), effective thirty days from the date of this order.

FRANKLIN D ROOSEVELT

The White House,

August 28, 1937.
