The PRESIDENT.

SIR: In the force employed in the office of the collector of customs at the port of   there are eight tellers who receive and count the money paid in at that office, amounting to $500,000 a day or upward, and who should be persons qualified to handle money with skill and to detect counterfeit coin and bills. One of these places is now vacant, and it is important that it should be filled at the earliest practicable date. The position is not one excepted from examination by  , clause 5; but the collector thinks that it would be imprudent and impracticable for him to be restricted in filling the vacancy to the three names that might be certified to him from the eligible register, and in this opinion the   concurs. But whether this class of positions and certain others in the customs service should be filled by noncompetitive examination or by special exception is a matter which the   has under consideration, but can not determine until after a visit to   and perhaps other ports. In view, however, of the necessity for immediately filling the present vacancy--but without establishing a precedent--the   has the honor to recommend that a noncompetitive examination for the purpose be authorized under subdivision (e), clause 2 of General Rule III,  .

Your obedient servants,

JNO.  ,

 .  ,

United States Civil Service Commissioners.

Approved,  .
