Executive Order 5096—Extending Port Limits of Boston, Massachusetts for Customs Port of Entry Purposes
April 19, 1929
Herbert Hoover

By virtue of the authority vested in me by the Act of Congress approved August 1, 1914, making appropriations for the sundry civil expenses of the Government for the fiscal year ended June 30, 1915, the port limits of Boston, Massachusetts, the headquarters port of Customs Collection District No. 4 (Massachusetts) are hereby extended to include Braintree, Weymouth and Hingham, and the waters adjacent thereto, effective thirty days from the date of this order.

HERBERT HOOVER

The White House,

April 19, 1929.
