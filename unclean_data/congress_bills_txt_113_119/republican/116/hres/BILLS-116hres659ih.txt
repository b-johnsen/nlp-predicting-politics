RESOLUTION
Affirming that States should maintain primacy for the regulation of hydraulic fracturing for oil
and natural gas production on State and private lands and that the
President should not declare a moratorium on the use of hydraulic
fracturing on Federal lands (including the Outer Continental Shelf), State
lands, private lands, or lands held in trust for an Indian Tribe unless
such moratorium is authorized by an Act of Congress.
That States should maintain primacy for the regulation of hydraulic fracturing for oil and natural gas production on State and private lands and that the President should not declare a moratorium on the use of hydraulic fracturing on Federal lands (including the Outer Continental Shelf), State lands, private lands, or lands held in trust for an Indian Tribe unless such moratorium is authorized by an Act of Congress.
