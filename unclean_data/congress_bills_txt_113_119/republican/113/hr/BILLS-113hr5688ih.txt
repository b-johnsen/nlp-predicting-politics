1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Stop Ebola Act
.
2.
Special immigration limitations relating to Ebola Virus Disease
(a)
In general
(1)
Visa restrictions
Beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, no consular officer may issue an immigrant or
nonimmigrant visa to any alien who is a citizen or national of any
affected country.
(2)
Admission restrictions
Beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, an alien is ineligible to be admitted into the
United States if the alienâs journey to the United States originated in,
or included transit through, any affected country.
(b)
Affected country defined
For purposes of this section, the term
affected country
means a country that has been designated by the World Health Organization as having
widespread
and intense transmissions
under the World Health Organization Ebola
Response Roadmap.
(c)
Other definitions
Except as provided in subsection (b), the terms used in this section shall have the meaning given
such terms in section 101(a) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8
U.S.C. 1101(a)).
