Whereas CVDs result in tremendous health care costs and lost productivity and, if not addressed, the United States alone will spend over $1,000,000,000,000 by 2035 on costs relating to CVD;
Whereas educational attainment, household income, residential environment, access to health care, and structural racism contribute significantly to disparities in CVD mortality in the United States;

Whereas essential quality-assured medicines and technologies are critical components to address the global burden of CVDs; and
