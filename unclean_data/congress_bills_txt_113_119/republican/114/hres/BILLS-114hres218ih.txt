RESOLUTION
Expressing the sense of the House of Representatives regarding the conditions for the United States
becoming a signatory to any international agreement on greenhouse gas
emissions under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change.
That it is the sense of the House of Representatives that the United States should not be a signatory to any protocol to, or other agreement regarding, the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change of 1992, at negotiations in Paris in December 2015, or thereafter, which wouldâ
(1)
mandate new commitments to limit or reduce greenhouse gas emissions for the Annex I Parties, unless the protocol or other agreement also mandates new specific scheduled commitments to limit or reduce greenhouse gas emissions for Non-Annex I Parties within the same compliance period;
(2)
not ensure that all parties to the agreement reduce an equal amount of greenhouse gas emissions at an equivalent rate within the same compliance period;
(3)
result in serious harm to the economy of the United States; and
(4)
not adequately protect United States intellectual property rights.
