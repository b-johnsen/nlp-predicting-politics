RESOLUTION
To express support for recognition of June 2018 as National Orca Protection Month.
Whereas orcas are an icon of the Pacific Northwest;
Whereas orcas are culturally and spiritually important to Indian tribes in the Pacific Northwest;
Whereas the Puget Sound is home to three pods of Southern Resident orcas;
Whereas the Southern Resident orca population has declined dramatically over the past 50 years;
Whereas the Southern Resident orcas are among the most contaminated marine mammals in the world;
Whereas the Southern Resident orcas are threatened by declining chinook salmon populations;
Whereas the Southern Resident orcas have been protected under the Endangered Species Act of 1973 (
16 U.S.C. 1531
et seq.) since 2005 and are one of the most endangered marine mammal populations in the world;
Whereas the Southern Resident orca population reached its lowest level in 34 years in 2018, with 13 fewer whales than when the population was listed under the Endangered Species Act of 1973;
Whereas June 2018 has been recognized by the States of Washington and Oregon as Orca Awareness Month; and
Whereas the Federal Government has an obligation to protect and restore the Southern Resident orca population: Now, therefore, be it
That the House of Representatives supports recognition of National Orca Protection Month.
