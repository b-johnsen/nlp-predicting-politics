CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
Designating September 2025 as
Gynecologic Cancers Awareness Month
.
Whereas gynecologic cancers include cervical, ovarian, endometrial/uterine, vaginal, and vulvar cancers with the first 3 being the most common;
Whereas there are approximately 118,920 new cases of these cancers diagnosed and approximately 34,630 deaths from gynecologic cancers annually;
Whereas endometrial cancer is 1 of the few cancers with rising incidence and mortality rates;
Whereas endometrial and ovarian cancer rank fifth and sixth in cancer deaths among women, respectively;
Whereas symptoms are often vague or misunderstood, and there are no screening tests to identify endometrial and ovarian cancers before they progress to later stages;
Whereas cervical cancer is the only gynecologic cancer with a reliable screening test and is vaccine-preventable;
Whereas increased awareness of gynecologic cancers can lead to earlier diagnosis, followup with appropriate health professionals, and improved outcomes; and
