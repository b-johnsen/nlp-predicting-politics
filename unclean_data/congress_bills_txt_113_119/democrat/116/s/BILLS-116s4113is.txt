1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Broadband Reform and Investment to Drive Growth in the Economy Act of 2020
or
BRIDGE Act
.
2.
Findings
Congress finds the following:
(1)
Access to affordable, reliable broadband is essential to full participation in modern life in the United States.
(2)
The persistent
digital divide
in the United States is a barrier to the economic competitiveness of the United States and equitable distribution of essential public services, including health care and education.
(3)
In many communities across the country, increased competition among broadband providers has the potential to offer consumers more affordable, high-quality options for broadband service.
(4)
The digital divide disproportionately affects communities of color, lower-income areas, and rural areas.
