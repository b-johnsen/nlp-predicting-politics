1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Illegal Red Snapper Enforcement Act
.
2.
Methodology for identifying the country of origin of red snapper imported into the United States
(a)
Definitions
In this section:
(1)
Administrator
The term
Administrator
means the Administrator of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.
(2)
Appropriate committees of Congress
The term
appropriate committees of Congress
meansâ
(A)
the Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate; and
(B)

(3)
Under Secretary
The term
Under Secretary
means the Under Secretary of Commerce for Standards and Technology and the Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology.
(b)
Standard methodology for identification
The Under Secretary and the Administrator, in consultation with the Commissioner of U.S. Customs and Border Protection and the Commandant of the Coast Guard, shall jointly develop a standard methodology based on chemical analysis for identifying the country of origin of red snapper imported into the United States thatâ
(1)
is consistent with the needs of Federal and State law enforcement agencies in combating illegal, unreported, and unregulated fishing;
(2)
minimizes processing time; and
(3)
involves the use of a field kit that can be easily carried by one individual.
(c)
Report
Not later 2 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the Under Secretary shall submit to the appropriate committees of Congress a report that includes the following:
(1)
A summary of the methodology developed under subsection (b).
(2)
A plan for operationalizing the methodology.
(3)
Recommendations for further research on identification methods, including other potential applications of the methodology.
