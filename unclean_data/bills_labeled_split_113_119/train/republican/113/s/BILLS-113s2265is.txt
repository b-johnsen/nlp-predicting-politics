1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Stand with Israel Act of 2014
.
2.
Prohibition on foreign assistance
(a)
In general
Except as provided under subsection (b) and notwithstanding any other provision of law, no amounts
may be obligated or expended to provide any direct
United States assistance, loan guarantee, or debt relief to the
Palestinian Authority, or any affiliated governing entity or leadership
organization.
(b)
Exception
The prohibition under subsection (a) shall have no effect for a fiscal year if the President
certifies to Congress during that fiscal year
that the Palestinian Authority hasâ
(1)
formally recognized the right of Israel to exist as a Jewish state;
(2)
publicly recognized the state of Israel;
(3)
renounced terrorism;
(4)
purged all individuals with terrorist ties from security services;
(5)
terminated funding of anti-American and anti-Israel incitement;
(6)
publicly pledged to not engage in war with Israel; and
(7)
honored previous diplomatic agreements.
