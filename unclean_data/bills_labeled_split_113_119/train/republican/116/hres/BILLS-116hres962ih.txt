Whereas the United States Agency for International Development reports that East Africa is currently suffering the worst locust outbreak in decades, which will devour crops and pasture across that region;
Whereas there are more than 25 million people experiencing severe food insecurity in Ethiopia, Kenya, Somalia, South Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda and Sudan, and untold more will be at risk if the locusts, currently in their most vulnerable state, are not controlled using airborne pesticides, before they mature into full grown locusts;
Whereas the World Food Programme (WFP) reports that there may be insufficient resources to conduct aerial spraying to address the outbreak of desert locusts that is currently devastating East Africa;
Whereas the locust situation in the Horn of Africa, particularly in Kenya, Ethiopia, and Somalia represents an unprecedented threat to food security and livelihoods;
Whereas a relatively small investment now to help eradicate the locusts will obviate or ameliorate the need for a much larger United States emergency aid effort later;
