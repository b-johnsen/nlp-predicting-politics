1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Stop Taxpayers Obligations to Perpetrators of Sexual Harassment Act
.
2.
Prohibiting use of public funds for payment of settlements and awards under Congressional Accountability Act in sexual harassment claims involving Members of Congress
(a)
Prohibition
Section 415 of the Congressional Accountability Act of 1995 (
2 U.S.C. 1415
) is amendedâ
(1)
in subsection (a), by striking
subsection (c)
and inserting
subsections (c) and (d)
; and
(2)
by adding at the end the following new subsection:
(d)
No use of public funds for payments of awards and settlements in connection with acts of sexual
harassment committed by Members of Congress
No funds of the Treasury of the United States may be used for the payment of an award or settlement under this Act in connection with a violation of section 201(a)(1) if the violation consists of an act of sexual harassment committed personally by a Member of the House of Representatives (including a Delegate or Resident Commissioner to the Congress) or a Senator.
.
(b)
Effective date
The amendment made by subsection (a) shall apply with respect to settlements and awards paid on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
