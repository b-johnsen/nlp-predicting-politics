3.
Independent advisory commission on labor automation
(a)
Establishment
The Secretary of Labor shall establish an independent advisory commission on labor automation to advise the Secretary on matters relating to jobs and occupations at risk of elimination as a result of automation.
(b)
Membership
The Secretary shall ensure that membership on the advisory commission established under subsection (a) includes individuals with expertise in labor, individuals with expertise in technology, and individuals with expertise in business.
(c)
Annual report
Not less frequently than annually, the advisory commission established under subsection (a) shall submit to the Secretary and make available to the public a report describing jobs and occupations at risk of elimination as a result of automation that includesâ
(1)
an identification of the States most affected by that risk; and
(2)
recommendations for collaboration with State workforce agencies to identify and address that risk.
(d)
Automation defined
In this section, the term
automation
means using technology to produce a good or service previously produced by human work.
