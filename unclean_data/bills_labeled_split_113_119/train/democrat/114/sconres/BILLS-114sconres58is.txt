Whereas an estimated 5,000,000 United States children have, or have had, a parent in prison or jail;
Whereas phone calls make it easier for families of incarcerated individuals to maintain positive relationships with their loved ones who are incarcerated;
Whereas phone calls help to reduce recidivism and promote the well-being of children;
Whereas a reduction in recidivism rates by just 1 percent would save United States taxpayers $250,000,000 per year in correctional costs;
Whereas families of incarcerated individuals frequently experience financial hardship because of the loss of a key wage earner;
Whereas the cost of maintaining contact with incarcerated loved ones through in-person visits can be prohibitive;
Whereas written correspondence, especially with small children and disabled individuals, can be an inadequate way of maintaining communication; and
