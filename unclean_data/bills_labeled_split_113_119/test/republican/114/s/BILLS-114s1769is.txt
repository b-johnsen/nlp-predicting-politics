Placement of United States Fish and Wildlife Service law enforcement officer in each African
elephant range country
(a)
In general
The Secretary, in coordination with the Secretary of State, may station in each African country that has a significant population of African elephants one United States Fish and Wildlife Service law enforcement officer in the primary United States diplomatic or consular post.
(b)
Duties
A United States Fish and Wildlife Service law enforcement officer stationed in an African elephant range country under subsection (a) shallâ
(1)
assist local wildlife rangers in the protection of African elephants; and
(2)
facilitate the apprehension of individuals who illegally kill, or assist the illegal killing of, African elephants.
