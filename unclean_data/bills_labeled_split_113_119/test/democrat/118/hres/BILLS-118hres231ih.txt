1.
Mandatory medical emergency preparedness training
(a)
In general
Rule XXIX of the Rules of the House of Representatives is amended by adding at the end the following:
5.
(a)
Each Member, Delegate, Resident Commissioner, officer, and employee of the House of Representatives shall complete a medical emergency preparedness training offered by the Office of the Attending Physician, which shall include training inâ
(1)
cardiopulmonary resuscitation; and
(2)
the use of an automated external defibrillator.
(b)
(1)
Each Member, Delegate, Resident Commissioner, officer, and employee of the House shall complete the training under this clause not later than 90 days after beginning service to the House, and annually thereafter.
(2)
The Office of the Attending Physician shall provide certification of the completion of the training established by this paragraph.
.
(b)
Effective date
(1)
In general
This resolution and the amendments made by this resolution shall take effect on the date that is 90 days after the date of the adoption of this resolution.
(2)
Training deadline for current Congress
Not later than 90 days after the effective date specified in paragraph (1), an individual who is serving as a Member, Delegate, Resident Commissioner, officer, or employee of the House of Representatives as of such effective date shall complete the medical emergency preparedness training required under clause 5(a) of Rule XXIX of the Rules of the House of Representatives, as added by this resolution.
