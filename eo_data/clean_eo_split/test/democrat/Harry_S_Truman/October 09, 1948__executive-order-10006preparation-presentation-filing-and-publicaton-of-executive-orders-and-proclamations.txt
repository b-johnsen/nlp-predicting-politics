By virtue of authority vested in me by   (49 Stat. 500, as amended; 44 U.S.C. 301 et seq.), and as President of  , it is hereby ordered as follows:

1. The following regulations shall govern the preparation, presentation, filing, and publication of Executive orders and proclamations, and shall constitute   1.91 through 1.97 of Chapter I of Title 1 of the Code of Federal Regulations:

§ 1.91 Form. Proposed Executive orders and proclamations shall be prepared in accordance with the following requirements:

(a) The order or proclamation shall be given a suitable title.

(b) The authority under which the order or proclamation is issued shall be cited in the body thereof.

(c) Punctuation, capitalization, orthography, and other matters of style shall, in general, conform to the most recent edition of the Style Manual of the United States Government Printing Office.

(d) The spelling of geographic names shall conform to the most recent official decisions of   established pursuant to the act of  , 61 Stat. 456 (43 U.S.C. 364 et seq.).

(e) Descriptions of tracts of land shall conform, so far as practicable, to the most recent edition of  ," published by, or other the direction of, the Director of  .

(f) Proposed Executive orders and proclamations shall be typewritten on paper approximately 8 x 12 ½ inches, shall have a left-hand margin of approximately 1 ½ inches and a right-hand margin of approximately 1 inch, and shall be double-spaced, except that quotations, tabulations, and descriptions of land may be single-spaced.

§ 1.92 Routing and approval of drafts. A proposed   order or proclamation shall first be submitted, with seven copies thereof, to the Director of  . If the Director of   approves it, he shall transmit it to the Attorney General for his consideration as to both form and legality. If the Attorney General approves it, he shall transmit it to the Director of  ,  . After determining that it conforms to the requirements of § 1.91 and is free from typographical or clerical error, the Director of   shall transmit it and three copies thereof to the President. If it is disapproved by the Director of   or by the Attorney General, it shall not thereafter be presented to the President unless it is accompanied by a statement of the reasons for such disapproval.

§ 1.93 Routing of originals and copies; seal. If the order or proclamation is signed by the President, the original and two copies thereof shall be forwarded to the Director of   for appropriate action in conformity with the provisions of  : Provided, That the seal of   shall be affixed, pursuant to direction of the President, to the originals of all proclamations prior to such forwarding.

§ 1.94 Numbering and certification.   shall number chronologically all Executive orders and proclamations and shall cause to be placed upon the copies thereof the following notation, to be signed by the Director or by some person authorized by him: "Certified to be a true copy of the original".

§ 1.96 Publication and distribution.   shall publish currently in the FEDERAL REGISTER all Executive orders and proclamations and shall supervise the promulgation and distribution thereof.
