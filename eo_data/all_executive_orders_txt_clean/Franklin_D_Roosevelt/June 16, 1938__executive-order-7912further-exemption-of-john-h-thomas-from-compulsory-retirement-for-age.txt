Executive Order 7912—Further Exemption of John H. Thomas From Compulsory Retirement for Age
June 16, 1938
Franklin D. Roosevelt

Whereas

, in my judgment, the public interest requires that John H. Thomas, Assistant Solicitor and member of the Board of Appeals, Department of the Interior, who was exempted from compulsory retirement for age for a period one year ending September 30, 1938, by Executive Order No. 7637 of June 19, 1937, be further exempted from such compulsory retirement for a period of one year:

Now, Therefore

, by virtue of and pursuant to the authority vested in me by section 204 of the act of June 30, 1932, 47 Stat. 382, 404 (U.S.C., title 5, sec. 715a), I hereby further exempt the said John H. Thomas from compulsory retirement for age for a period of one year ending September 30, 1939.

FRANKLIN D. ROOSEVELT

The White House,

June 16, 1938.
