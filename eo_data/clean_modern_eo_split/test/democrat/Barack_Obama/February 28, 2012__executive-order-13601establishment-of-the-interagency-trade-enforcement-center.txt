Section 1.

Policy.

Robust monitoring and enforcement of   rights under international trade agreements, and enforcement of domestic trade laws, are crucial to expanding exports and ensuring   workers, businesses, ranchers, and farmers are able to compete on a level playing field with foreign trade partners. To strengthen our capacity to monitor and enforce   trade rights and domestic trade laws, and thereby enhance market access for   exporters, executive departments and agencies (agencies) must coordinate and augment their efforts to identify and reduce or eliminate foreign trade barriers and unfair foreign trade practices to ensure that   workers, businesses, ranchers, and farmers receive the maximum benefit from our international trade agreements and under domestic trade laws.
