3. Section 167 of the Consular Regulations is amended to read as follows:

167.

Fees.

A fee of $1 must be collected for executing every application for a passport, and a fee of $5 must be collected for every passport issued with the following exceptions:

No fee should be collected for passports issued to officials or employees of   proceeding abroad in the discharge of their official duties or to members of their immediate families, or to seamen, or to widows, children, parents, brothers, and sisters of   soldiers, sailors, or marines buried abroad, whose journey is undertaken for the purpose and with the intent of visiting the graves of such soldiers, sailors, or marines, which facts shall be made a part of the application for a passport.

A fee of $2 must be collected for the renewal of every passport, except in the cases of persons mentioned in the preceding paragraph who maintain the same status as held by them at the time of the issue of their passports without fee and who are abroad for the same purpose for which their passports were issued.— Act of   (41 Stat. 750); act of  , as amended by the act of   (Public, No.   Cong.)
