By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of  , and to ensure appropriate agency disclosure of information, and consistent with the goals of section 552 of title 5, United States Code, it is hereby ordered as follows:

Section 1. Policy.

(a) The effective functioning of our constitutional democracy depends upon the participation in public life of a citizenry that is well informed. For  , the Freedom of Information Act ( ) has provided an important means through which the public can obtain information regarding the activities of Federal agencies. Under the  , the public can obtain records from any   agency, subject to the exemptions enacted by the   to protect information that must be held in confidence for the Government to function effectively or for other purposes.

(b)   requesters are seeking a service from   and should be treated as such. Accordingly, in responding to a   request, agencies shall respond courteously and appropriately. Moreover, agencies shall provide   requesters, and the public in general, with citizen-centered ways to learn about the   process, about agency records that are publicly available (e.g., on the agency's website), and about the status of a person's   request and appropriate information about the agency's response.

(c) Agency   operations shall be both results-oriented and produce results. Accordingly, agencies shall process requests under the   in an efficient and appropriate manner and achieve tangible, measurable improvements in   processing. When an agency's   program does not produce such results, it should be reformed, consistent with available resources appropriated by the   and applicable law, to increase efficiency and better reflect the policy goals and objectives of this order.

(d) A citizen-centered and results-oriented approach will improve service and performance, thereby strengthening compliance with the  , and will help avoid disputes and related litigation.

 . 3. Review, Plan, and Report.

(a) Review. Each agency's Chief FOIA Officer shall conduct a review of the agency's   operations to determine whether agency practices are consistent with the policies set forth in section 1 of this order. In conducting this review, the Chief FOIA Officer shall:

(i) evaluate, with reference to numerical and statistical benchmarks where appropriate, the agency's administration of the  , including the agency's expenditure of resources on   compliance and the extent to which, if any, requests for records have not been responded to within the statutory time limit (backlog); (ii) review the processes and practices by which the agency assists and informs the public regarding the   process;

(iii) examine the agency's:

(A) use of information technology in responding to   requests, including without limitation the tracking of   requests and communication with requesters;

(B) practices with respect to requests for expedited processing; and

(C) implementation of multi-track processing if used by such agency;

(iv) review the agency's policies and practices relating to the availability of public information through websites and other means, including the use of websites to make available the records described in section 552(a)(2) of title 5, United States Code; and

(v) identify ways to eliminate or reduce its   backlog, consistent with available resources and taking into consideration the volume and complexity of the   requests pending with the agency.

(b) Plan.

(i) Each agency's Chief FOIA Officer shall develop, in consultation as appropriate with the staff of the agency (including the FOIA Public Liaisons), the Attorney General, and the   Director, an agency-specific plan to ensure that the agency's administration of the   is in accordance with applicable law and the policies set forth in section 1 of this order.  . 7. General Provisions.

(a) The agency reviews under section 3(a) of this order and agency plans under section 3(b) of this order shall be conducted and developed in accordance with applicable law and applicable guidance issued by the President, the Attorney General, and the   Director, including the laws and guidance regarding information technology and the dissemination of information.
