Executive Order  —Establishing an Emergency Board To Investigate a Dispute Between   and Its Employees Represented by  
 
 

A dispute exists between   and certain of its employees represented by  .

The dispute has not heretofore been adjusted under the provisions of the Railway Labor Act, as amended (45 U.S.C. 151 et seq.) (the "Act").

A party empowered by the Act has requested that the President establish a second emergency board pursuant to section 9A of the Act (45 U.S.C. 159a).

Section 9A(e) of the Act provides that the President, upon such request, shall appoint a second emergency board to investigate and report on the dispute.

Now, Therefore, by the authority vested in me as President, by the Constitution and the laws of  , including section 9A of the Act, it is hereby ordered as follows:

Section 1. Establishment of the Board. There is established effective 12:01 a.m.,  , on  , a board of three members to be appointed by the President to investigate this dispute. No member shall be pecuniarily or otherwise interested in any organization of railroad employees or any carrier. The board shall perform its functions subject to the availability of funds.

 . 2. Report. Within   after creation of the board, the parties to the dispute shall submit to the board final offers for settlement of the dispute. Within   after submission of final offers for settlement of the dispute, the board shall submit a report to the President setting forth its selection of the most reasonable offer.

 . 3.  . As provided by section 9A(h) of the Act, from the time a request to establish a board is made until   after the board makes its report, no change, except by agreement, shall be made by the parties in the conditions out of which the dispute arose.

 . 4. Records Maintenance. The records and files of the board are records of   and upon the board's termination shall be maintained in the physical custody of  .

 . 5. Expiration. The board shall terminate upon submission of the report provided for in section 2 of this order.
