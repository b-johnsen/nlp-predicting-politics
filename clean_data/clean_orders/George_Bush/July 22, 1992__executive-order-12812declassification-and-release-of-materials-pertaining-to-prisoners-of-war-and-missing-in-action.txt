Executive Order 12812—Declassification and   to Prisoners of War and Missing in Action
 
 WHEREAS, the  , by  . 324 of  , has asked that I "expeditiously issue an   order requiring all executive branch departments and agencies to declassify and publicly release without compromising   national security all documents, files, and other materials pertaining to POWs and MIAs;" and

WHEREAS, indiscriminate release of classified material could jeopardize continuing   efforts to achieve the fullest possible accounting of  -era POWs and MIAs; and

WHEREAS, I have concluded that the public interest would be served by the declassification and public release of materials pertaining to  -era POWs and MIAs as provided below;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of  , I hereby order as follows:

Section 1. All executive departments and agencies shall expeditiously review all documents, files, and other materials pertaining to   POWs and MIAs lost in Southeast Asia for the purposes of declassification in accordance with the standards and procedures of Executive Order No.  .

 . 2. All executive departments and agencies shall make publicly available documents, files, and other materials declassified pursuant to section 1, except for those the disclosure of which would constitute a clearly unwarranted invasion of personal privacy of returnees, family members of POWs and MIAs, or other persons, or would impair the deliberative processes of the executive branch.

 . 3. This order is not intended to create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable by a party against  , its agencies or instrumentalities, its officers or employees, or any other person.
