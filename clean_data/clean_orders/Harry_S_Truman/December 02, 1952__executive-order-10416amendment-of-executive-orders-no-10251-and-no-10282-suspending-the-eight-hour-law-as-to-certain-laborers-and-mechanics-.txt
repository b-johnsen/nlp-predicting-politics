Executive Order  —Amendment of Executive Orders No.   and No.  , Suspending the Eight-Hour Law as to   and   by   and   Repectively
 
 

By virtue of the authority vested in me by section 1 of the act of  , 27 Stat. 340, as amended by the act of  , 37 Stat. 726 (40 U.S.C. 321), and as President of  , it is ordered that Executive Order No. 10251 of June 7,  , and Executive Order No.   of  , suspending the eight-hour law with respect to laborers and mechanics employed by   and  , respectively, on public work essential to the national defense, be, and they are hereby, amended as follows:

The provisio in the penultimate paragraph of each of the said orders fixing the rate of pay for overtime thereunder is amended to read as follows:

"Provided, that the wages of all laborers and mechanics so employed shall be computed on a basic day rate of eight hours of work with overtime to be paid at a rate not less than time and one-half for all hours of work in excess of eight hour in any one day."

HARRY S. TRUMAN
