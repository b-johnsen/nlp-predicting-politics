Purpose

. The national and economic security of   depends on the reliable and efficient functioning of critical infrastructure. Since   made   available worldwide, positioning, navigation, and timing ( ) services provided by space-based systems have become a largely invisible utility for technology and infrastructure, including the electrical power grid, communications infrastructure and mobile devices, all modes of transportation, precision agriculture, weather forecasting, and emergency response. Because of the widespread adoption of   services, the disruption or manipulation of these services has the potential to adversely affect the national and economic security of  . To strengthen national resilience,   must foster the responsible use of   services by critical infrastructure owners and operators.

Definitions

. As used in this order:

(a) "  services" means any system, network, or capability that provides a reference to calculate or augment the calculation of longitude, latitude, altitude, or transmission of time or frequency data, or any combination thereof.

(b) "Responsible use of   services" means the deliberate, risk-informed use of   services, including their acquisition, integration, and deployment, such that disruption or manipulation of   services minimally affects national security, the economy, public health, and the critical functions of  .

(c) "Critical infrastructure" means systems and assets, whether physical or virtual, so vital to   that the incapacity or destruction of such systems and assets would have a debilitating impact on national security, national economic security, national public health or safety, or on any combination of those matters.

(d) "  profile" means a description of the responsible use of   services—aligned to standards, guidelines, and sector-specific requirements—selected for a particular system to address the potential disruption or manipulation of   services.
