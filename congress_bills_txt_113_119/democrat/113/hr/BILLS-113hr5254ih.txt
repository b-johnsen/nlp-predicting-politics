(b)
Adverse findings
(1)
In general
The head of an agency shall not award a bonus to an employee of the agency until 5 years after the
end of the fiscal year in which the Inspector General or another senior
ethics official of the agency or the Comptroller General of the United
States makes an adverse finding relating to the employee.
(2)
Previously awarded bonuses
If the Inspector General or another senior ethics official of the agency or the Comptroller General
of the United States makes an adverse finding relating to an employee, the
head of the agency employing the employee, after notice and an opportunity
for a hearing, shall issue an order directing the employee to repay the
amount of any bonus awarded to the employee during the year during which
the adverse finding is made.
