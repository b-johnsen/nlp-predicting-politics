340.
Grants for vehicles with hot and cold food storage for meal delivery
(a)
In general
The Assistant Secretary shall award grants in accordance with subsection (c) on a competitive basis to States receiving a grant under section 331 or 336 to enable such States to purchase, customize, or repair vehicles meeting the requirement under subsection (b) to be used in delivering meals to older individuals through a nutrition project supported under either such section.
(b)
Vehicle requirement
The requirement under this subsection is that the vehicle has the capability to safely store hot food and cold food, as determined by the Assistant Secretary, while delivering meals.
(c)
Application and selection process
(1)
Applications
(A)
In general
A State receiving a grant under section 331 or 336 that seeks a grant under this section shall submit an application to the Assistant Secretary at such time, in such manner, and containing such information as the Assistant Secretary may reasonably require, including the demonstrations described in subparagraph (B).
(B)
Demonstrations
An application under this paragraph shall contain a demonstrationâ

(ii)
that a program is in effect within the State, on the date on which the State submits the application, to use vehicles meeting the requirement under subsection (b) to provide nutrition services under section 331 or 336.
(2)
Selection
The Assistant Secretary, in selecting States meeting the requirements under paragraph (1) to receive a grant under this section, shall give preference toâ
(A)
States with a high percentage of individuals who are 60 years of age or older; and
(B)
States with a high rate of individuals at or below the poverty line.
(d)
Grant amount
A grant awarded under this section shall be for an amount that is equal to or less than $3,000,000.
(e)
Authorization of appropriations
There is authorized to be appropriated $60,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2020 through 2024 to carry out this section.
