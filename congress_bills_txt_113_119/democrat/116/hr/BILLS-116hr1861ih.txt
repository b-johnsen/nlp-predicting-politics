Congress finds the following:
(1)
The Secretary of Education has not formally issued policy guidance on school start times.
(2)
Eight to 10 hours of sleep a day is recommended for teenagers aged 13 to 18 years, but early school start times are contributing to lack of sleep among adolescents.
(3)
Despite the shift in biological rhythms during puberty, which causes adolescents to sleep later at night and wake later in the morning, 93 percent of high schools and 83 percent of middle schools in the United States started before 8:30 a.m. in 2014.
(4)
