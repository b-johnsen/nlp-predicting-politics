1.
Short title
This Act may be cited as the
Stopping Corrupt Actors from Making Political Action Committees Act
or the
SCAM PAC Act
.
2.
Prohibition on disbursement of funds to entities owned or controlled by individuals with authority over operations of political committees
(a)
In general
Section 302 of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971 (
52 U.S.C. 30102
) is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:
(j)
(1)
A political committee may not make disbursements to an entity owned or controlled in whole or in part by an individual, or by an immediate family member of an individualâ
(A)
who is authorized to carry out executive or managerial authority over the operation of the committee;
(B)
who is authorized to solicit or disburse funds for or on behalf of the committee; or
(C)
who is an employee of the committee (whether paid or unpaid) and who provides the committee with professional services (other than accounting or legal services) relating to the committeeâs campaign or fundraising strategy.
(2)
A political committee may not employ or allow to volunteer on behalf of the committee an individual who owns or controls an entity that has accepted disbursements made from any political committee in violation of paragraph (1).
(3)
In this subsection, the term
immediate family member
means, with respect to an individual, a parent, parent-in law, grandparent, child, child-in law, grandchild, spouse, or sibling.
.
(b)
Effective date
The amendment made by subsection (a) shall apply with respect to disbursements made on or after the date that is 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act.
