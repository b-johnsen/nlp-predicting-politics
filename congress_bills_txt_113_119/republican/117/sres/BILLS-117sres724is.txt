RESOLUTION
Expressing the sense of the Senate that the historic definition of a recession is 2 negative quarters of gross domestic product growth.
Whereas, globally, the most commonly used and widely accepted definition of recession is 2 negative quarters of gross domestic product growth;
Whereas most of the recessions in the United States identified by the National Bureau of Economic Research have consisted of 2 negative quarters of gross domestic product growth and in each of the last 10 instances in which there have been 2 or more such quarters, a recession was officially declared;
Whereas use of the historic definition of a recession by the Federal Government has been important for purposes of economic study and analysis, historic comparisons, and policy making: Now, therefore, be it
That it is the sense of the Senate that the Federal Government should continue to use the historic definition of a recession.
