5.
Congressional notification with respect to exceptional access lending
The Bretton Woods Agreements Act (
22 U.S.C. 286â286xx
), as amended by section 2 of this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following:
74.
Congressional notification with respect to exceptional access lending
The United States Executive Director at the International Monetary Fund may not support any proposal that would alter the criteria used by the Fund for exceptional access lending if the proposal would permit a country that is ineligible, before the proposed alteration, to receive exceptional access lending, unless, not later than 30 days before consideration of the proposal by the Board of Executive Directors of the Fund, the Secretary of the Treasury has submitted to the Committee on Financial Services and the Committee on Foreign Affairs of the House of Representatives and the Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs and the Committee on Foreign Relations of the Senate a report on the justification for the proposal and the effects of the proposed alteration on moral hazard and repayment risk at the Fund.
.
6.
Monitoring and technical assistance
Nothing in this Act shall be interpreted as requiring the opposition of the United States to the provision of monitoring, technical assistance, or advisory services by the International Monetary Fund.
