(a)
Affirmative defense
(1)
In general
Chapter 1

28.
Affirmative defense for certain criminal violations
(a)
Definition
In this section, the term
forcible felony
includes treason, murder, manslaughter, sexual battery, carjacking, home-invasion robbery, robbery, burglary, arson, kidnapping, aggravated assault, aggravated battery, aggravated stalking, aircraft piracy, unlawful throwing, placing, or discharging of a destructive device or bomb, and any other felony which involves the use or threat of physical force or violence against any individual.
(b)
Use or threatened use of non-Deadly force in defense of person
(1)
In general
It shall be an affirmative defense to a prosecution under this title that the defendant used, threatened to use, or attempted to use force, except deadly force, against an aggressor if, and to the extent that, the defendant reasonably believed that the use, threat to use, or attempt to use such force was necessary to defend the defendant or another individual against the imminent use of unlawful force by the aggressor.
(2)
No duty to retreat
For purposes of paragraph (1), an individual who is at risk of becoming a victim of a forcible felony shall not be required to first seek retreat as the preferred alternative to using force to act in self-defense.
